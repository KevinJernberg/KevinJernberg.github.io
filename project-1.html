In Missing Picture you play as a private detective in 1980s western Sweden. Where you have been sent to investigate the supernatural dissapperence of 3 teenagers. By using your polaroid camera, you snap photos of the evidence to be used later when figuring out what happend in this forest. Missing Picture was Nominated for Best Narrative in Sweden Game Awards 2024 and was nominated for Sk√∂vde Academic Game Awards at Sweden Game Conference 2024.

This project would allow me to test myself as a Game Developer and as a Programmer. The teams ambitions were high, and as 1 of the 3 programmers on this project. My skills in interdisciplinary communication, rapid digital prototyping and the Unity Game Engine was put to the test.

My Contributions

For this project i wanted my technical knowledge about computer rendering, 3D-Math and Unity to not only benefit my work but also to benefit the final game as a whole. I also strove to write more maintainable and well structured code, as we predicted that a lot of testing needed to be done to get the Polaroid Camera right.

Polaroid Camera

I broke down the functions of the camera to:

  1. 1. Input
  2. 2. Take Photo
  3. 3. Apply Photo / Output

The input was the thing we was most unsure about, in the start we just wanted the camera to just take a photo when the left mouse button was pressed. However, after player feedback said we wanted a more authentic camera feel. A redesign would need to be done.

The redesign included that you actually look into the camera, then snap a photo. As we had built the input based upon events and functions this was trivial to change, as we just swapped the animation and only let the player photo when the camera was looked into.

From the outside, the Read Image part might be the most complicated. However, the Take Photo and output part is the least convoluted. As we just render a frame from an invisible scene camera. Apply the render to an image, then apply the image to a sprite. The Output (Sprite) can then be used in a material on the polaroid photo game object.

Rendering

As we did not have a technical artist on the team, i took the initiative to create shaders and handle post processing. By using Unity's Post Processing volumes and Shader Graphs i made pixel shaders that made the world feel a little more alive.
